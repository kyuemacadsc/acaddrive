Academic Resources

Hey! Welcome to the KY Students' Resources - Here we collaborate on notes, essays, worked solutions, links to useful websites, book recommendations and etc. That being said, use these resources with caution. Enjoy!

Academic Sharing

The slides used by the academic department for a presentation or a sharing session will be placed in this folder for further future reference of the students, teachers and staffs.

Admission Test

Less popular tests aren't updated as often. We try to keep the big tests (STEP, ENGAA, BMAT, LNAT) updated. Though, there really is so many of them that it is often difficult to keep track.

Essay Bank/Personal Statement

Essays provided based on subjects that is collected from teachers and students. Bear in mind that different teachers may mark essays leniently/strictly. Besides that, plagiarism will hurt you more than it will benefit you more. So be wise, we trust in your intellectual maturity.

Interview Recordings

It is true that interviews are known to be one of the most harrowing experience to face especially for University application, we have compiled a number of senior recordings in this folder for future reference of the students.

KY Past Papers

The KY past papers have, at times, included questions atypical of CAIE A Levels. They can be a lot harder than CAIE Past Papers, and so are not representative of the difficulty of the actual exams.

AS & A Levels Past Papers

Like any other examinations be it IGCSE or SPM - A Levels lie on the same principle of "practice makes perfect". In this folder is a compilation of A Level CAIE past year papers ranging across a wide array of subjects.

Textbook Worked Solutions

Official PDF files of worked solutions for A Levels textbooks that can be quite helpful when checking the answers. The subjects included are Further Mathematics and others.

SAT Material

The folder contains a number of useful SAT materials that can be used as preparation for the American University Admissions Tests. The materials will be updated from time to time.

Notes

The notes provided are mostly by students. The notes have not been vetted by an expert, however it is more than reliable enough as reference. Besides, syllabus tend to change over time, so it's important to keep track whether the notes provided are in line with the latest syllabus. Lastly, the submitters still retain the right to edit or retract their notes. Therefore, it is recommended for you to retain your own notes making these notes as reference only.
